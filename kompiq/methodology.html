The Science

Built on research, not guesswork

The Kompiq Assessment draws on decades of research in organizational psychology, personality science, and career development. Here is how it all fits together.

Format

Why forced-choice questions

The format of an assessment determines the quality of its data. Kompiq uses forced-choice for specific, research-backed reasons.

The problem with Likert scales

Most assessments use "rate yourself from 1 to 5" questions. Research consistently shows that these scales suffer from social desirability bias. People rate themselves higher on traits they value and lower on traits they do not, regardless of their actual behavior.

Forced-choice format eliminates this problem by presenting two equally positive statements. You cannot "game" the assessment because both options are valid. The pattern of your choices across 120 questions reveals your genuine work tendencies.

Eliminates Social Desirability

Research shows that forced-choice formats reduce social desirability bias by 40 to 60 percent compared to Likert scales. You answer based on genuine preference, not perceived "right" answers.

Surfaces Trade-offs

Real work involves constant trade-offs. Speed versus accuracy. Autonomy versus collaboration. Forced-choice mirrors the decisions you make every day, producing more ecologically valid data.

Reduces Acquiescence Bias

On Likert scales, many people tend to agree with everything. Forced-choice removes the "agree/disagree" axis entirely, so each response carries genuine signal about your preferences.

Higher Predictive Validity

Studies in organizational psychology demonstrate that forced-choice assessments predict job performance and satisfaction more accurately than traditional self-report measures. The trade-off format captures how people actually make decisions.

120 questions: why this number

Each of the 10 domains requires a minimum number of data points to produce a reliable score. With fewer than 10 questions per domain, the margin of error becomes too large. With more than 15 per domain, you hit diminishing returns and increased respondent fatigue.

120 questions across 10 domains gives an average of 12 questions per domain, with some questions measuring cross-domain interactions. This produces reliable dimensional scores while keeping the assessment under 25 minutes for most people.

The 10 Domains

Research basis for each dimension

Each of the 10 domains in the Kompiq framework is grounded in established research. Here is the scientific foundation behind each one.

01
Cognitive Style

How you process and structure information

Research in cognitive psychology has identified consistent individual differences in how people process information. Some people default to analytical, sequential processing. Others rely on pattern recognition and intuition. Studies in decision science show these differences are stable across contexts and strongly predict job fit and performance in knowledge work.

02
Energy Patterns

What sustains versus depletes you

Research on burnout and engagement shows that energy management is distinct from skill. You can be highly skilled at something that drains you. Studies on person-job fit demonstrate that alignment between energy patterns and job demands predicts long-term engagement better than competence alone.

03
Communication Mode

How you share and receive information

Communication research identifies stable preferences in how people transmit and process ideas. These include directness versus diplomacy, verbal versus written preference, and think-then-speak versus think-by-speaking patterns. Mismatches in communication mode between team members are one of the leading causes of workplace friction, according to organizational behavior research.

04
Decision Architecture

How you make choices under uncertainty

Research in behavioral economics and decision science has documented consistent individual differences in decision-making. Speed versus deliberation, data reliance versus intuition, reversibility tolerance, and risk appetite are all measurable traits that directly affect how well someone performs in different role types.

05
Team Dynamics

Your natural role in groups

Research on team composition shows that individuals adopt consistent roles in group settings. These roles are distinct from job titles. Studies on high-performing teams demonstrate that performance depends more on role balance than individual talent. Kompiq measures your natural team tendency: driver, facilitator, challenger, supporter, or specialist.

06
Motivation Drivers

What actually makes you start and sustain effort

Motivation research distinguishes between what people say motivates them and what their behavior reveals. Self-determination theory identifies three core drivers: autonomy, competence, and relatedness. Kompiq measures how these drivers interact with your specific work patterns, producing a motivation profile that predicts sustained engagement better than self-reported preferences.

07
Learning Orientation

How you acquire knowledge and adapt

Research on learning styles has evolved significantly. Current evidence supports the idea of learning preferences (how you prefer to learn) rather than fixed styles. Kompiq measures your preferred learning mode: reading, doing, observing, discussing, or teaching. It also measures your generalist versus specialist orientation, which predicts career trajectory in rapidly changing fields.

08
Time Relationship

How you relate to deadlines, planning, and pacing

Research on temporal orientation shows consistent differences in how people plan, prioritize, and pace their work. Some people are natural planners who thrive with structure and advance timelines. Others work best in reactive, deadline-driven environments. Placing a planner in a reactive role, or vice versa, creates chronic friction that skill cannot overcome.

09
Stress Response

How you perform under pressure

Research on occupational stress shows that individuals have distinct, stable patterns in how they respond to pressure. Some people perform better under moderate stress while others degrade quickly. Studies on resilience and recovery demonstrate that stress response patterns predict career longevity and satisfaction more reliably than skill assessments.

10
Value Alignment

What organizational cultures fit your wiring

Person-organization fit research consistently shows that alignment between personal values and organizational culture predicts job satisfaction, commitment, and retention. You can have the perfect role at the wrong company and still feel misaligned. Kompiq measures your value orientation across multiple culture dimensions to identify where you will and will not thrive.

Scoring

How scoring works

Your responses go through a multi-layer scoring process that captures both your individual patterns and the relationships between them.

Weighted pattern analysis

Each question contributes to one or more domain scores with specific weights. Questions that measure the intersection of two domains carry split weights. The weighting system was developed through iterative testing to maximize the predictive accuracy of each dimensional score.

Your raw scores are then normalized against the broader response population to produce percentile rankings. This tells you not just where you fall on each dimension, but how your profile compares to others.

Cross-domain interaction mapping

Your dimensions do not exist in isolation. The scoring system identifies meaningful interactions between your domain scores that create emergent patterns. A high Cognitive Style score combined with low Time Relationship structure creates a different work profile than high Cognitive Style with high Time Relationship structure.

These interaction patterns are what make each report unique. Two people with identical single-domain scores can receive very different reports because their cross-domain patterns diverge.

Primary Scores

Raw dimensional scores on each of the 10 domains, calculated from your direct responses to questions mapped to that domain.

Interaction Scores

Derived from the relationships between your primary scores. These capture how your dimensions amplify, moderate, or create tension with each other.

Strength Extraction

Your 15 ranked strengths are extracted from the combination of primary and interaction scores, identifying the specific capabilities that emerge from your unique profile.

Fit Matching

Your complete profile is compared against environment and role archetypes to identify where your wiring naturally fits and where it will create friction.

AI Personalization

How AI generates your report

Scoring produces numbers. The AI engine turns those numbers into actionable narrative that speaks directly to your work experience.

Beyond templates

Traditional assessments use a template library: if you score "high" on a trait, you get the "high" paragraph. This creates reports that feel generic because they are. Kompiq uses AI to generate narrative that accounts for your complete profile, not just individual score ranges.

The AI engine receives your full scoring output, including primary scores, interaction patterns, and derived strengths. It then generates each section of your report as a cohesive narrative that references specific patterns in your data. The result reads like a report written about you specifically, because it was.

Profile-Aware Generation

The AI receives your complete scoring profile before generating any text. Every paragraph accounts for your full pattern, not isolated scores.

Cross-Reference Consistency

Sections reference each other. Your stress profile connects to your environment fit. Your strengths connect to your role recommendations. The AI maintains consistency across the entire report.

Actionable Language

The AI is trained to produce specific, actionable guidance rather than abstract descriptions. Instead of "you are creative," you see "you generate ideas through verbal brainstorming and perform best when given a sounding board."

Behavioral Specificity

Each insight includes behavioral indicators: what the pattern looks like in practice, when it helps, when it may create friction, and what you can do about it.

Quality

Validity and reliability

An assessment is only useful if it measures what it claims to measure and produces consistent results.

Our approach to validity

Kompiq uses multiple approaches to ensure assessment quality. Content validity is maintained through systematic question development grounded in established research frameworks. Each question maps to specific dimensional constructs with clear theoretical justification.

Construct validity is supported by the consistent factor structure observed across respondent populations. The 10 domains produce statistically distinct scores, confirming that they measure separate constructs rather than variations of the same trait.

Test-retest consistency

When the same person takes the assessment at different times, their dimensional scores remain stable. This is expected because the patterns Kompiq measures reflect enduring work tendencies, not temporary states or moods.

Minor score variations across administrations fall within expected ranges and do not materially change the report's conclusions or recommendations.

Report Sections

Nine sections, zero filler

Each section of your report serves a specific purpose. Together, they give you a complete operating manual for your professional life.

01

10-Domain Analysis

Your report opens with a detailed breakdown of how you scored across all 10 dimensions: Cognitive Style, Energy Patterns, Communication Mode, Decision Architecture, Team Dynamics, Motivation Drivers, Learning Orientation, Time Relationship, Stress Response, and Value Alignment.

Each domain includes a score visualization, a percentile indicator showing where you fall relative to the broader population, and a personalized narrative explaining what your score means in practical terms.

Score Visualizations Percentile Rankings Personalized Narratives
02

15 Ranked Strengths

Not a generic list. Your strengths are extracted from the intersection of your dimensional scores and ranked by intensity. Each strength comes with a description of how it shows up in your work, specific behavioral examples, and strategies for amplifying it.

You might see something like "Pattern Recognition Under Pressure" ranked above "Structured Communication" because your combination of cognitive style and stress response scores created that specific strength profile. The ranking reflects your unique wiring, not a pre-made template.

Ranked by Intensity Behavioral Examples Amplification Strategies
03

Blind Spots

Your report identifies 8 to 10 patterns that may be working against you. These are not weaknesses in the traditional sense. They are tendencies that serve you well in some contexts but create friction in others.

For each blind spot, you get a clear explanation of when it helps, when it hurts, and a specific mitigation strategy you can start using immediately. This section tends to produce the most "I never realized that" moments.

8 to 10 Patterns Context-Specific Mitigation Strategies
04

Environment Fit

This section maps the work settings where your wiring thrives. It covers pace (fast versus measured), structure (high versus low), team size, reporting relationships, and physical environment preferences.

You will see specific descriptions like "You perform best in environments where you have autonomy over your schedule but clear expectations on deliverables." This is not personality typing. It is a practical guide for evaluating your next role or restructuring your current one.

Pace Preferences Structure Needs Team Configuration
05

Roles That Fit You

Based on your combined profile, the report recommends specific types of roles that align with your strengths, energy patterns, and stress tolerance. These are not job titles pulled from a database. They are role archetypes described in terms of the actual work involved.

You might see "roles that involve translating complex information for non-technical audiences" or "positions where you own a process end-to-end with minimal committee oversight." Each recommendation is tied back to your dimensional scores so you understand why it fits.

Role Archetypes Strength Alignment Energy Sustainability
06

Stress Profile

This is one of the sections that sets Kompiq apart. Most assessments skip stress entirely. Your stress profile covers how you respond under pressure, what triggers your stress response, how you recover, and what environments will chronically drain you.

You will learn your "breakdown signals," which are the early warning signs that stress is affecting your performance before you consciously notice it. Your report also provides specific recovery strategies matched to your wiring, not generic advice about meditation or exercise.

Pressure Response Breakdown Signals Recovery Strategies
07

Culture Fit Map

Your value alignment and communication scores combine to create a map of the organizational cultures that match your wiring. This goes beyond "startup versus corporate." It covers decision-making culture, feedback norms, meeting cadence, and leadership style expectations.

The section includes red flags to watch for during interviews, along with specific questions you can ask to assess whether a company's culture actually matches what they claim. These questions are generated from your specific profile, so they probe the areas that matter most to your performance.

Culture Indicators Red Flags Interview Questions
08

90-Day Growth Plan

Your report does not stop at describing who you are. It gives you a concrete plan for what to do with this information. The 90-day growth plan identifies three priority development areas and provides weekly practices for each.

Week 1 might focus on observing a specific pattern at work. Week 4 might introduce a new behavior. Week 8 might involve gathering feedback. Each practice is small, specific, and builds on the previous one. Progress indicators help you track whether the practices are working.

3 Priority Areas Weekly Practices Progress Indicators
09

Team Guide: "How to Work With You"

The final section is a shareable one-pager designed for your manager, direct reports, or close colleagues. It summarizes your communication preferences, decision-making style, stress signals, ideal collaboration mode, and what you need from your team to do your best work.

This is not your full report. It is a distilled version that gives others a practical manual for working with you effectively. Many Kompiq users report that sharing this guide improved their working relationships more than anything else in the report.

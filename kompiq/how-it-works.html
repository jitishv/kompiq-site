The Process

How the Kompiq Assessment actually works

From the first question to your completed report, here is exactly what happens and why each step matters for your career clarity.

The Assessment

What happens during the assessment

You will answer 120 forced-choice questions over 18 to 22 minutes. Each question is designed to surface a specific pattern in how you work.

01

You receive your unique assessment link

After purchase, you get an email with a private link to your assessment. There is no account to create and no software to install. The assessment runs in your browser on any device.

You can start immediately or save the link for later. The link does not expire.

02

120 questions, two choices each

Every question shows you two statements. Both are positive. Both are valid. Your job is to choose which one fits you better on a sliding scale.

For example, you might see "I prefer to plan projects in detail before starting" paired with "I prefer to figure things out as I go." Neither is right. Neither is wrong. The pattern of your choices across 120 questions is what builds your profile.

03

Go at your own pace

Most people finish in 18 to 22 minutes. There is no timer, no countdown, and no penalty for taking longer. You can pause and return using the same link.

The best results come from going with your gut rather than overthinking. Your instinctive response is usually the most accurate one.

04

Your report generates automatically

Once you submit your final answer, the system begins building your report. This takes just a few minutes. You will receive an email notification as soon as it is ready.

Every paragraph is written specifically for your answer pattern. Two people with similar scores can receive very different reports because the system accounts for the relationships between domains, not just individual scores.

Scoring

How your responses become your profile

The scoring system looks beyond your individual answers. It analyzes the patterns, relationships, and trade-offs across all 120 questions.

Dimensional Scoring

Each of your 120 responses contributes to one or more of the 10 dimensions. Some questions feed into a single dimension while others measure the intersection of two or three dimensions at once.

Pattern Detection

The system identifies clusters in your responses. If you consistently choose action over analysis, speed over thoroughness, and autonomy over guidance, those patterns combine into a coherent work style profile.

Cross-Domain Analysis

Your score on one dimension affects how other dimensions are interpreted. For instance, high stress tolerance combined with low energy recovery creates a different profile than high stress tolerance with high energy recovery.

Strength Ranking

From your dimensional scores and cross-domain patterns, the system extracts and ranks your top 15 professional strengths. These are not generic labels. They are specific to how your dimensions interact.

Environment Matching

Your combined profile is compared against a library of work environment types, role characteristics, and culture patterns. The system identifies which settings match your wiring and which ones will drain you.

AI Personalization

Once scoring is complete, the AI report engine takes your raw profile and generates narrative sections that explain your patterns in plain language. Every section references your specific response tendencies.

Report Generation

How your report is built

Your report is not assembled from templates. It is generated from your unique data using a multi-stage process.

Stage 1: Raw Scoring

Your 120 responses are converted into dimensional scores, trait indicators, and pattern clusters. This creates the numerical foundation of your profile.

Stage 2: Profile Synthesis

The system analyzes relationships between your dimensional scores. It identifies where your strengths cluster, where tension exists between competing drives, and where your blind spots may live.

Stage 3: Narrative Generation

AI generates personalized narrative sections for each part of your report. These are written in second person and reference your specific patterns, not generic descriptions of score ranges.

Stage 4: Recommendations

Based on your synthesized profile, the system generates role recommendations, environment fit analysis, and your 90-day growth plan with weekly practices tailored to your top development areas.

Stage 5: Team Guide

A shareable one-page guide is created that translates your profile into actionable guidance for colleagues and managers. This covers communication style, decision preferences, and collaboration tips.

Stage 6: Final Assembly

All sections are compiled into your complete report with visualizations, charts, and actionable summaries. You receive a link to view it online, with the option to export as PDF.

Getting the Most From It

How to read your report

Your report contains a lot of information. Here is how to approach it for maximum value.

Tip 01

Read the summary first

Your report opens with a one-page profile summary. This gives you the big picture before you get into the detail of each domain. Read this first and sit with it before going deeper.

Tip 02

Focus on the surprises

Some sections will confirm what you already know about yourself. That is good. But the real value is in the sections that surprise you. Pay extra attention to blind spots and cross-domain tensions.

Tip 03

Re-read after a week

Your first reading captures the obvious insights. A second reading, after you have had time to observe yourself at work, will reveal patterns you missed the first time through.

Tip 04

Share the team guide

The team guide at the end of your report is designed to be shared. Send it to your manager, your direct reports, or close colleagues. The feedback you get will multiply the value of the assessment.

Tip 05

Start the growth plan immediately

Your 90-day growth plan includes weekly practices. Start in the first week after receiving your report. The plan works best when you build momentum early rather than waiting for the "right" time.

Tip 06

Use it in career decisions

When evaluating a new role, promotion, or team change, revisit your environment fit and culture fit sections. These are your decision filters. If a role does not match your wiring, it will cost you more energy than it returns.

FAQ

Common questions about the process

How long does the Kompiq Assessment take?
Most people complete the 120 questions in 18 to 22 minutes. There is no timer, so you can take as long as you need. Going with your first instinct on each question tends to produce the most accurate results.
What is a forced-choice assessment?
A forced-choice assessment presents two positive statements and asks you to choose which one fits you better. This format eliminates the tendency to pick socially desirable answers because both options are equally valid. It surfaces your genuine work patterns rather than what you think the "right" answer should be.
Can I pause and come back later?
Yes. Your progress is saved automatically. You can close your browser and return to the same link whenever you are ready. Most people prefer to complete it in a single sitting, but there is no requirement to do so.
When do I get my report?
Your personalized report is generated within minutes of completing the assessment. You receive an email with a link to view it online. You can also save it as a PDF directly from your browser.
Can I retake the assessment?
The assessment is designed to be taken once. Your professional patterns are relatively stable, so retaking it would produce very similar results. If your circumstances change significantly, contact hello@kompiq.com to discuss options.
Is my data private?
Yes. Your responses and report are completely private. Kompiq does not share individual data with employers or third parties. Your results belong to you alone.
What devices can I use?
The assessment works on any modern browser. Desktop, laptop, tablet, or phone. For the best experience, a screen larger than a phone is recommended, but the assessment is fully responsive.
